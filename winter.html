Sravani Kotha "does Chekov tonight"

Ivanoff Act 3- Anton Chekov

Winter Scene - Outside IVANOFF'S house. The snow-covered landscape stretches as far as the eye can see. SHABELSKI and LEBEDIEFF, bundled up in warm clothes, engage in conversation. BORKIN stands nearby, shivering in the cold.

LEBEDIEFF:

The wind is biting today, my friend. I wish I had brought an extra scarf.

SHABELSKI:

Indeed, it is cold. I can feel it in my bones. How about a hot cup of tea to warm us up?

BORKIN:

I should have worn thicker socks. This frost is unbearable!
